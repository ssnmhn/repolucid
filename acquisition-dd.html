The diligence gap in every small deal

On a $50k–$500k acquisition, the economics of hiring a CTO-for-a-day never work — so non-technical buyers verify revenue, skim the demo, and pray about the code. That's where the surprises live:

the classic one

Founder-shaped code

One person wrote 95% of it, documented nothing, and is about to leave with the earn-out. We measure this — commit history doesn't lie.

the expensive one

License landmines

A single copyleft dependency, or "proprietary" code that's a fork of someone's GPL project, can change what you're allowed to do with your own product.

the quiet one

Maintenance debt

Abandoned frameworks, no tests, five years of "temporary" fixes. Fine until your first must-ship change. Priced into the deal — or into your next year.

FAQ

I'm under LOI and the clock is ticking. How fast, really?

48 hours from repo access for the $499 memo, four days for the Deep Memo. Rush needs are exactly why this service exists — say so in the request and we'll confirm the timeline before you commit anything.

The seller won't share the code before closing. Now what?

Common and solvable: sellers routinely grant read-only access under NDA during exclusivity — it's a standard DD request, and a seller who refuses it entirely is itself a signal. We'll give you the exact wording to ask with, and the free Red-Flag Scan works from the public listing alone.

How can an AI's opinion be worth $499?

It reads every file — something no human does at any price point you'd pay on a small deal — and each claim cites evidence you can verify or hand to any engineer later. Then the guarantee does the rest: if the memo doesn't materially inform your decision, you don't pay. Read the Umami sample and judge the depth yourself.

Will you tell me whether to buy?

We'll tell you what's true about the technology, rank the risks, and arm you with the seller questions. The buy decision weighs price, market, and your plans — that's yours. What we promise: you won't be surprised by the code after closing.

What about the rest of diligence — financials, contracts, churn?

Out of scope, on purpose; use your accountant and lawyer there. We cover the one pillar the usual small-deal team can't: the asset's actual code, infra posture, and the technical claims in the listing.
